Selecting Seeds: Choose ripe mangoes from a healthy tree. Remove the seed from the fruit, making sure it's clean and free from any pulp. Seed Preparation: Remove the hard outer shell of the seed to expose the inner kernel. You can carefully use a knife to do this, but be cautious not to damage the inner kernel.

You can germinate the seed by placing it in a shallow dish of water or directly plant it in a pot with soil. Planting mango seeds If using water, suspend the seed halfway in the water with the pointed end facing downwards. Change the water every few days to prevent stagnation and mold. If planting directly in soil, plant the seed about an inch deep with the pointed end facing down. Keep Moist Ensure that the seed or soil remains consistently moist but not waterlogged. Germination Time Germination typically takes 2-4 weeks, though it can vary.

Transplanting: Once the seed has sprouted and the seedling has a few leaves, transplant it into a larger pot or directly into the ground if the climate allows. Soil: Use well-draining soil with organic matter. Mangoes prefer slightly acidic soil with a pH of around 5.5 to 7.5. Sunlight: Place the seedling in a location where it receives plenty of sunlight, at least 6-8 hours a day. Watering: Keep the soil consistently moist, especially during the first year of growth. However, avoid waterlogging, as mangoes are susceptible to root rot.

Pruning: Regular pruning issential for shaping the tree, promoting air circulation, and removing dead or diseased branches. Prune during the dormant season (winter) to avoid interfering with fruit production. Pest and Disease Control: Keep an eye out for common pests like mango scale, mango shoot caterpillar, and mango hopper. Treat infestations promptly with appropriate insecticides. Also, watch for fungal diseases such as anthracnose and powdery mildew, and use fungicides as needed.

Fertilization Schedule: Mango trees benefit from regular fertilization, especially during the growing season (spring and summer). Use a balanced fertilizer with a higher potassium content (e.g., 10-10-10 or similar NPK ratio) or a fertilizer specifically formulated for fruit trees. Apply fertilizer every 2-3 months during the growing season, starting in the second year of growth. Scatter the fertilizer evenly Fertilizer Application around the drip line of the tree (the area under the outermost branches). Water the area thoroughly after applying fertilizer to help it penetrate the soil.

Depending on the mango variety, fruits typically mature 3-6 months after flowering. Harvest ripe fruits by gently twisting them or using pruning shears.
