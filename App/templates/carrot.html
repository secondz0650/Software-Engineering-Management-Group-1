Carrots, scientifically Daucus carota, are valued for their sweet taste and vibrant color. Thriving in temperate climates, carrots come in various shapes and sizes, with orange being the most common color.

- Choose carrot varieties suited to your region and soil type.
- Consider factors such as length, shape, and color preferences.
- Popular varieties include Danvers, Nantes, Imperator, and Chantenay.

- Carrots are typically grown from seeds.
- Sow carrot seeds directly into the garden soil, as they do not transplant well.
- Plant seeds ¼ to ½ inch deep in well-prepared, loose soil.
- Ensure the soil is free of rocks and debris to prevent misshapen roots.
- Keep the soil consistently moist but not waterlogged for optimal germination.
- Carrot seeds usually germinate within 7-14 days, depending on soil temperature and moisture levels.

- Thin out seedlings once they have developed several true leaves to ensure proper spacing.
- Space carrot seedlings 2-3 inches apart in rows spaced 12-18 inches apart.
- Water seedlings regularly, ensuring the soil remains evenly moist but not waterlogged.
- Mulch around seedlings to help retain soil moisture and suppress weeds.

- Carrots prefer loose, well-drained soil with a pH between 6.0 and 6.8.
- Keep the soil consistently moist throughout the growing season, especially during dry periods.
- Weed regularly to prevent competition for nutrients and water.
- Avoid deep cultivation near carrot plants to prevent damage to the shallow roots.

- Carrots have relatively low fertilizer requirements compared to other crops.
- Incorporate compost or well-aged manure into the soil before planting to provide nutrients.
- Avoid excessive nitrogen fertilization, as it can result in forked or hairy roots.
- Side-dress with a balanced fertilizer (such as 10-10-10) once or twice during the growing season if necessary.

- Carrots are typically ready for harvest 60-80 days after planting, depending on the variety.
- Harvest carrots when they reach the desired size and color. Most varieties are ready when the roots are about ¾ to 1 inch in diameter.
- Gently loosen the soil around the carrots with a garden fork or shovel, being careful not to damage the roots.
- Pull the carrots from the soil by grasping the foliage near the base and pulling straight up.
- Trim the tops and wash the carrots thoroughly before storing them in a cool, humid environment.
