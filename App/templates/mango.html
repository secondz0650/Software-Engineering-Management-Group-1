Pruning: Regular pruning issential for shaping the tree, promoting air circulation, and removing dead or diseased branches. Prune during the dormant season (winter) to avoid interfering with fruit production. Pest and Disease Control: Keep an eye out for common pests like mango scale, mango shoot caterpillar, and mango hopper. Treat infestations promptly with appropriate insecticides. Also, watch for fungal diseases such as anthracnose and powdery mildew, and use fungicides as needed.

Fertilization Schedule: Mango trees benefit from regular fertilization, especially during the growing season (spring and summer). Use a balanced fertilizer with a higher potassium content (e.g., 10-10-10 or similar NPK ratio) or a fertilizer specifically formulated for fruit trees. Apply fertilizer every 2-3 months during the growing season, starting in the second year of growth. Scatter the fertilizer evenly Fertilizer Application around the drip line of the tree (the area under the outermost branches). Water the area thoroughly after applying fertilizer to help it penetrate the soil.
